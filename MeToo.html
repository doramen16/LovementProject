#MeToo

You Are Not Alone.

In 2007, an era where hashtags didn’t exist, Tarana Burke pioneered the Me Too campaign.
Burke, the founder and director of the youth organization Just Be Inc., started the Me Too
campaign as a grass-roots movement to reach sexual assault survivors in underprivileged communities.
The phrase “Me Too” was to be used between survivors in order to let them know that they weren’t alone in the healing process. It was a symbol of hope that a movement for radical healing could be made possible through solidarity. The campaign developed into a hashtag when actress Alyssa Milano encouraged her followers on Twitter to share their stories of sexual harassment and assault using the hashtag #MeToo.
